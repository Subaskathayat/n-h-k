My Culinary Odyssey

From Himalayan village kitchens to the world's most prestigious dining rooms

The Journey

1998

The Spice Box Awakening

At age 12, I discovered my grandmother's ancient spice box in our Uttarakhand home. The aromas of cardamom, star anise, and mountain herbs became my first culinary language.

2005

Apprenticeship at The Oberoi

My professional journey began under Chef Hemant Oberoi, where I learned the discipline of classical French techniques applied to Indian ingredients.

2012

Michelin Stars in Barcelona

Three transformative years at Tickets, absorbing Albert Adrià's molecular gastronomy while developing my "Deconstructed Spice Route" concept.

2016

Tokyo's Kaiseki Revelation

Studying under Master Chef Yamamoto revealed the profound connection between seasonality, presentation, and mindfulness in cuisine.

2020

Returning to Roots

Establishing my Mumbai studio kitchen to reinterpret Himalayan cuisine through a global lens, earning recognition from the James Beard Foundation.

The Spice Route Manifesto

"I don't fuse cuisines—I reveal their hidden conversations. When saffron meets sansho pepper, they're simply rediscovering their ancient kinship."

01. Respect the Origin

Every ingredient has a terroir. Our smoked paprika comes from the same Spanish family farm for 8 years.

02. Technique as Translation

Using sous-vide for goat curry isn't modernization—it's unlocking flavors our ancestors couldn't access.

03. Memory on the Plate

The crackle of our onion bhaji foam replicates the sound of monsoon rains on my childhood tin roof.

My current spice collection - 147 varieties from 23 countries
